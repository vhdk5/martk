56. Mentions

Monitoring mentions around the web can be resolved in a manner of ways:

1) Using a free tool such as Google Alerts

2) Using a premium tool such as Awario

Google Alerts

You can receive an e-mail whenever a new result appears online for a topic that you’ve selected. You’ll create a topic you want to monitor, such as your brand or product name, and Google will alert you when there’s news, announcements, articles, or mentions of your name.
This data is hard to use in a qualitative sense, but you can evaluate the frequency of alerts and use the alerts to evaluate other avenues to capture data. If you see a new blog article written about your brand, you’ll know to take a closer look at referral traffic to evaluate if that mention was valuable.

To create an alert

  1. Go to Google Alerts
  2. In the box at the top, enter a topic you want to follow.
  3. To change your settings, click Show options. You can change:
    • How often you get notifications
    • The types of sites you’ll see
    • Your language
    • The part of the world you want info from
    • How many results you want to see
    • What accounts get the alert
  4. Click Create Alert. You’ll get emails whenever we find matching search results.

Awario

Awario gives you the ability to monitor conversations happening about your brand in real-time. It gives you the ability to answer questions such as:

  • What is my target audience?
  • How does my target audience differ from my competitor’s?
  • Are our competitors’ products more popular than ours?
  • What is more trendy: X or Y?
  • What is the impact of the competitive product launch on my brand?

Awario helps provide quantitative metrics on this qualitative data. The collect this data and present it in various graphs and tables.

You can compare mentions between two brands, to help understand the impact of a campaign, or to evaluate trends.

Capturing data about mentions online isn’t always the most intuitive, but it’s a valuable way to measure how your brand or product is spreading.
