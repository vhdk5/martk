26. Common Facebook & Twitter Data

Facebook

There are two primary sets of data you will be retrieving from Facebook:

Data surrounding your business page, which includes metrics such as:

  • Actions on Page

    • Demographic data
    • Page Views
    • Page Likes
    • Post Reach
    • Post Engagements
    • Video Views

  • Data surrounding the advertisements you’re running, which includes metrics such as:

    • Impressions
    • Spend
    • CPC
    • Performance
    • Engagements (CPE)

As you look at the summary data, you’ll encounter three common terms:

   Page views: The number of times a Page's profile has been viewed by people.

   Reach: The number of people who had any content from your Page enter their screen.

   Impressions: The number of times any content from your Page entered a person's screen.

You can click in to any section to reveal deeper insights. For example, you could click on Posts to generate a summary of the performance of the content you are posting on Facebook.

You could then click on a specific post to gather deeper insights about that content.

A common term here that you’ll encounter is Engagement. This is the total number of likes and shares on your post.

Facebook also provides the ability to download this data in a manner that is usable in Excel, Google Sheets, or to be imported into other analytic software. From the Insights Overview simply select Export Data.

When running paid promotions, you will have access to the Ad Center attached to your Facebook Page, or the Ads Manager.

The Ad Center gives you simple tools to boost your content, or boost your page. It also includes relatively straightforward reporting:

Here you can see how many people your advertisement reached, the clicks on any links, how much you spent, and drill into basic areas such as Actions, People, Placements, and Locations.

  Actions: Shows you specifically how many conversions, clicks, & views received

  People: Shows you gender and age range data

  Placements: Shows where on Facebook your ad was delivered

  Locations: Show you which states or regions saw the ad

The Ads Manager is a robust advertising platform, where the majority of large campaigns are created. If you are running paid campaigns, it’s here that you will gather most of your data.

You will have the ability to click into any of the ads you are running to see metrics such as:

  • Performance
    • Reach
    • Frequency
    • Impressions
    • Amount Spent
  • Cost
    • CPM
    • CPC
    • CPE
  • Media
    • Videos Plays
    • Video Watches to 25%, 50%, 75%, 100%
  • Clicks
    • Clicks
    • CTR
  • Sales
    • Conversions

You can also dive into the specific Reporting section to:

  • Create reports using flexible breakdowns and drag-and-drop columns.
  • Pivot, sort and filter to drill into your data and surface actionable insights.
  • Saved reports get refreshed automatically so data and exports are always up-to-date.

Twitter provides ample reporting data at analytics.twitter.com. There you can gather a summary of your account performance across metrics such as:

  • Tweets
  • Tweet Impressions
  • Profile Visits
  • Mentions
  • Followers

You’ll also get an at-a-glance look at your Top Tweet, Top Mention, and Top Follower for the last 3 months.

You can drill into the specific sections similar to Facebook:

The Tweets section allows you to look at deeper metrics surrounding the content you’ve published. You’ll be able to look at:

  • Engagement Rate
  • Link Clicks
  • Retweets
  • Likes
  • Replies

Twitter provides ample demographic data on your audience, with the Audience section. You’ll have access to data such as:

  • Interests
  • Behaviors
  • Mobile Carrier
  • Household Income
  • Gender
  • Net Worth
  • Marital Status
  • Education
  • Occupation
  • Consumer Buying Styles
  • Home Ownership

To view insights related to your Paid marketing on Twitter, you’ll use ads.twitter.com. Much like Facebook, you’ll have access to all the paid metrics you’d expect to see.

All the same paid marketing data is available, with the opportunity to drill deeper into each section. You’ll have the ability to export the data so you can use it in a manner that makes the most sense for your analytics project.
