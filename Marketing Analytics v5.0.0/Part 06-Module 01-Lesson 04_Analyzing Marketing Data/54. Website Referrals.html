54. Website Referrals

Every visitor to your site, came from somewhere. We call this a “referral”. Referral traffic can come from sources like facebook.com, a blog article, or even your newsletter.

The easiest place to measure referral traffic is inside of Google Analytics. You’ll access this report by visiting Acquisition -> All Traffic -> Referrals

Here you can see the where traffic was referred to your site from, and evaluate it against metrics such as sessions, time on site, or conversion performance if you have conversion tracking setup.

When you look at referral traffic you want to evaluate not only the sites driving traffic, but if that traffic is driving engagement. You can look at how many pages per session to understand if visitors are looking at more content on your site, or the average time on page to determine if the content resonates with what they were hoping to see.

For example, by sorting this table for Avg. Session Duration (time on site), we can reveal a variety of insights.

Here we see that several referral sources have a lower than average session duration. We can see the avg. duration is 3 min and 33 seconds, and Quora and Facebook are delivering visitors who don’t stay on the page for very long.

When a visitor arrives to your page from a referral source, they have an expectation that needs to be met. They clicked because they have hopes for what information they will be presented with. If your time on site is a very low number, you’ll want to evaluate what is wrong with that content.

Referral data also helps you aggregate information from your social media platforms. You’ll be able to evaluate when traffic is arriving from your social media posts, what devices visitors are using, and how they behave on your site in relation to other sources.

You can also identify unique sources of traffic. For example, you may identify that a coupon website is driving a lot of traffic to your website. If that traffic is not generating conversions, you may consider looking at your listing on that website and updating the coupons that are available to encourage people to redeem them.

Referral reports allow you to measure the impact of traffic, whether it’s social, a PR placement, a new blog that wrote about you, or affiliates. By looking at user engagement, visitors, and goal completions, you can determine how well each site contributes to your ROI.

Ultimately, you should be thinking how to make this information actionable. Find high-performing referring websites to focus your future efforts on.
