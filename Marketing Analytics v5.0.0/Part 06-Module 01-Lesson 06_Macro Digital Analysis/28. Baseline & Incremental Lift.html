28. Baseline & Incremental Lift

Baselines are important to evaluate changes in specific marketing activities. Baseline is a value that exists outside of what is being measured in the campaign — it’s what would happened had you not done anything.

If the baseline baseline is the attributed value of everything outside of the marketing campaign being measured — or in simpler terms, what would have happened anyway.

Establishing a baseline allows you to model information in a more meaningful way when you’re measuring your marketing campaigns.  
If you have a baseline for your sales, you can look at the big picture and evaluate if a large multi-channel campaign had an impact. For example you could say “We increased sales 10% above our baseline as a result of the television campaign we ran”.

The baseline also helps isolate sales from the effects of other influences, such as seasonality or competitive promotions.

Baseline Sales or Base Volume is a function of marketing actions. Another commonly used term alongside Baseline is, Incremental Lift, which specifically means contemporaneous, immediate gains (lifts) resulting from factors such as price reductions, distribution fluctuations & seasonality. An incremental lift does not change the overall baseline.

When it comes time to measure our baseline, ideally we would use a control group to establish a baseline. However, it’s usually not cost-effective or time-effective to do so, which means we will turn to historical sales and adjust them for our expected growth, with adjustments for seasonal influences.

You start by looking at historical data for sales. You’ll separate those sales into baseline sales and incremental sales using a regression analysis.

From there, you work through forecasting growth and factoring in areas of seasonality. This is usually done by looking at areas where incremental lift happened in a predictable manner. For example, an increase in snack purchases near the super bowl.

Once you’ve evaluated just the baseline sales, and modeled how they grow, you can evaluate lift.

Lift % = Incremental Sales / Baseline Sales

After running a campaign, you’ll evaluate the amount of incremental sales generated, and when compared to baseline sales, you can model the percent lift.

There’s a lot of complex theory in a true baseline, and incremental lift model. However, it’s important that you’re aware of the foundations on how this model works and why it is used.
