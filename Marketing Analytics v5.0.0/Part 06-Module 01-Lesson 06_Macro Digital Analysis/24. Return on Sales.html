24. Return on Sales

Return on Sales (ROS) is how a company demonstrates if it has the ability to generate profits from its sales effort. We represent this value as a percentage, which effectively outlines the profit that results from each dollar of sales.

The math is relatively straightforward:

ROS = Operating Profit / Net Sales

This metric is useful because it helps you analyze the current performance of your business, and compare that performance to any other company regardless of their size. This way, you could evaluate if a small business is more efficiently turning a profit.

The return on sales equation measures the percentage of sales converted to income. This metric factors in all the overhead that goes into the sales, which is why we consider it to also be a metric of efficiency. If you have low operating expenses, you will have a higher ROS percentage.

Let’s say for example our Grocery Store generated $5 million in sales, and $250,000 in profits.

250,000 / 5,000,000 = 5%

This is a fairly low number, and suggests we might need to improve our margins. But, as we’ve learned together — metrics aren’t that great if they aren’t being compared. So, we could compare this metric to that of our industry, or competitors. If we find out the Grocery Industry averages 2-3% we could then consider our 5% to be far better than it looked initially.

ROS is most effective when it is measured multiple times, as it can vary as the company grows and as sales change.
