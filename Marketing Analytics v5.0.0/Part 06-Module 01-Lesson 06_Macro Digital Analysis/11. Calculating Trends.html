11. Calculating: Trends

To truly monitor market trends, you’ll need to run business experiments, buy industry reports, or review publicly available data. Much could be dedicated on the topic of truly evaluating market trends.

Business categories rise, and fall, so it is important that we keep a pulse on them. A relatively easy and straightforward approach to evaluating trends is through Google Trends: trends.google.com.

Rather than investing in complex reporting, you can simply provide topics to Google to evaluate how interested people are in a particular topic. You can use this analysis to make assumptions about the market as a whole, as people’s interest tend to be pretty strong indicators as to what is popular and what is not.

With Google Trends, you simply provide a query to see its trend line. You can also compare multiple queries to one another.

Each data point is divided by the total searches of the geography and time range it represents to compare relative popularity. Otherwise, places with the most search volume would always be ranked highest. This is important as you look at the trend data — this isn’t meant to tell you how many people are doing a search, but rather how that topic trends relative to all other searches.

Google trend results scaled on a range of 0 to 100 based on a topic’s proportion to all searches on all topics.

Trends help you understand if a market is growing, declining, or staying fairly neutral. Keep in mind, this isn’t a perfect analysis. For example, Google Trends is all about interest, and you may have a company that is spending a lot of time in the media, say Facebook for example. Would you consider social media as a trend to be in decline just because Facebook was showing a lot of interest? Of course not. So you’ll have to use your judgement when evaluating trends in this manner.

Let’s say you sell sheet music online for pianos. You can evaluate the overall trend of people searching for pianos, or sheet music. Here we can see this is a relatively stable market trend.

What about cryptocurrency? Significantly more volatile.

Take 10 minutes, and explore Google Trends. Evaluate trends for the following areas (or think up areas that interest you).

  • Car brands (Ford, Lexus, Toyota, Honda, Tesla, etc.)
  • Solar energy
  • Dog breeds (Poodle, Bulldog, Golden Retriever, etc.)
  • Sports (Football, Soccer, Baseball, etc.)
  • Fast food brands (McDonalds, Burger King, Wendy’s)
  • Social brands: Facebook, Twitter, LinkedIn
