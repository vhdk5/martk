07. Quiz: Layering in Data

Free Answer:

What data would you collect to answer the following:

QUESTION:

How popular was our store last Friday night?

ANSWER:

To answer this question, you likely would want to collect:

  • Number of Visitors to the store by hour
  • Revenue generated per hour

You could compare that data to historical trends to determine if the store was gaining popularity, or losing it.

What data would you collect to answer the following:

QUESTION:

What items do we sell the most of in October?

ANSWER:

List of all items sold in october
Qty of items sold in October

To answer this question we would look at data for all of October. We would look at the products we sold and how many of each. We could then look at the item that had the most sales, and determine that is the product we sell the most of.

What data would you collect to answer the following:

QUESTION:

How did our Sunday Paper coupon perform?

ANSWER:

Coupon redemptions
Average order amount
Average discount amount
Revenue generated for that period

To answer this question, we need to layer in multiple pieces of data. We need to count how many coupons were redeemed, and we need to figure out how that coupon performed. We’d likely want to look at the average order for folks using the coupon, vs those who do not use the coupon. Or, we might look at the overall revenue generated for that period and compare it to a similar period to determine if the coupon contributed to a lift in our overall sales. We could also look at the average discount amount, so we can determine if the money we spent acquiring the customer (the cost of the discount) generated a positive ROI.
