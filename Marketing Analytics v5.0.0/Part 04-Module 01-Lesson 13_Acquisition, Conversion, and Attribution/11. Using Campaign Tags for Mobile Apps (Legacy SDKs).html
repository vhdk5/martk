11. Using Campaign Tags for Mobile Apps (Legacy SDKs)

Tracking mobile app install campaigns and general app usage campaigns is not difficult, but it does require some coordination with your app developers and some attention to configurations within GTM, Google Analytics, and Firebase. Let’s take a look at mobile app campaign tracking in both legacy SDKs for IOS and Android as well as using GTM + Firebase SDK.

There are two types of mobile app campaign tracking:

  1. Install Campaigns: campaigns that drive users to install your mobile app from either the Google Play Store or the Apple Store

  2. General Campaigns: you can use marketing campaigns to drive users, who have already installed your mobile app to engage with your. These campaigns directly open or deep link into an already-installed app.

GTM + Firebase SDK Tracking

One significant advantage of using GTM + Firebase SDK for tracking mobile apps is the dynamic linking and deep linking functionality this enables.

Deep-linking: deep linking are web links that take a user into the screen hierarchy of your mobile apps. In other words, they drop a user directly on app content/functionality beyond the homescreen of your app and can increase in-app conversion rates, for example by bringing users closer to the actions they desire (placing an order, e.g.)

Dynamic Linking: if you are tracking mobile app usage in both Google Analytics and Firebase (a practice we recommend), then a very useful functionality is dynamic links. Dynamic links allow you to create a single campaign URL that automatically directs users to the appropriate app version or appropriate app store if they do not have your app installed already. This is extremely useful. For users that already have your app installed, it can take them to the respective iOS or Android deep-link you specify in the dynamic link setup. If they don’t have your app installed, you can have it take them to the Apple Store or the Google Play Store respectively to your app download/install option.
