07. Getting Oriented in Google Analytics

Getting oriented in Google Analytics

Here is a snapshot of the default landing page for Google Analytics for the Google Online Merchandise Store. By default, the analytics account opens up on the master view, you can change the view in the top left corner.

Navigation bar

On the left-hand side you will always find the navigation bar, which is helpful to find the right report or to re-orient yourself if you don’t remember which report you were looking at. Click each report type to expand the section, and then click on each headline to reveal additional details.

The navigation is organized in a few sections. We will primarily focus on Audience, Acquisition, Behavior and Conversion. These 4 sections align with the 4 lessons that follow. Each lesson, we will dig deeper into the reports in each one of these sections.

On the right of the navigation bar, you will find the reports. In this screenshot you are looking at the general overview report. There are a few other things to note that will help you get oriented:

Date Range

In the top right corner you will find the date range for your report. You can change this date range depending on what you are evaluating.

Graph Display

The graph on the page displays one metric by default, but you have the option to display a second metric and compare. By default, the graph will show you data day by day, but you can choose to make that more granular by choosing to display hourly data points on the graph, or less granular by choosing for instance weekly data.

Annotations

There is a little arrow under the graph. When you click that, you will see a space for annotations. You can add annotations, or notes to every data point in the graph. That may help you to remember later if something special happened at that particular time. For instance you could say: “big story in the NYTimes about the Merchandise store’. You can add annotations by double clicking on a datapoint.

Segments

The data are always reported for a certain audience. By default, Google Analytics shows you your total audience overview. But, it is possible to select a narrower segment you are interested in. You see the segment selection on top of the graph.

As you are navigating around Google Analytics, it is a good idea to always check for which segment you are displaying data. Once you select a particular segment, Google Analytics will display the data for just that segment unless you change it again. Google Analytics is sticky that way, and when you get deep into navigation, you may forget that you had selected a subset of your population, so keep that in mind._

Metrics

Below the graph, different metrics are shown. In this screenshot, the graph displays sessions. But, you could display any of these other metrics too. Note that you get a little teaser of what a trend graph for any of these metrics will look like, in a micro chart next to the metric. We will explain what the metrics mean in a little bit.

Dimensions

Below you will find some interesting dimensions that you can use to describe your data, or to get more insights into what is going on on your site. You see demographics, some information on the systems people use to access your site, and more info on the mobile platforms they access from.

New vs. Returning visitors

Note that there is also a graph that shows you the proportion of new vs. returning visitors for the time frame you are looking at.

At any point when you feel lost in Google Analytics, you can hover over the labels and Google Analytics will give you a definition. As you navigate through reports, you will also see question marks spread around which you can click to get some help or info on a report.
