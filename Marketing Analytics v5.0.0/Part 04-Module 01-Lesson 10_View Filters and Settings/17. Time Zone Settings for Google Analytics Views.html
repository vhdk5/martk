17. Time Zone Settings for Google Analytics Views

In the view that we created in the previous example, we set the time zone to align to the physical location of the organization. This is one of several approaches that you can take in setting the time zone in a new view, including:

Option 1: Align with the Time Zone Set in Your Google Ads Account

If you are using Google Ads, aligning your Google Analytics view time zone with the timezone of your Google Ads (previously called AdWords) account is important for synching day-level metrics across the platforms. This should perhaps be the overriding consideration when you're specifying the time zone for your Google Analytics view.

Option 2: Align with Your Organization's Physical Location

This could be a good option for a multinational organization that has country-specific subdomains or subdirectories tracked into the same Google Analytics property. In this case, you could create views filtered by hostname or subdirectory for each of the countries, and then apply the time zone that corresponds most closely with that country.

Option 3: Align with the Physical Location of the Majority of Your Users

This could be a suitable approach if the majority of your visitors are located within a single time zone. As another approach, you could choose option 1 or 2 above, and capture the time zone of your visitors as a session-scope custom dimension.
