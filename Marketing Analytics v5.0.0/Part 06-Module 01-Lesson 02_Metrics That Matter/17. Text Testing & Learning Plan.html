17. Text: Testing & Learning Plan

As you finalize the Analytic Brief, you come to the Testing & Learning Plan. The hope, is that through this exercise, you’ve evaluated where you have uncertainty. There may be data that is insufficient, funnel areas that are unrepresented, or shortfalls that were overlooked in the campaign planning.

You need to look at everything you’ve collected and figure out your plan for resolving uncertainty. Cesar A. Brea, in Pragmalytics, has three questions we should evaluate in this section:

  • What should we study further?
  • What data and analysis do we need?
  • What should we try?

Do not skip answering these questions. It’s easy to overlook this section, but you need to force yourself to think through these questions and truly understand the impact these answer may have on your approach.
 Oftentimes you’ll find a gap in your own analytics approach, or even gaps that the marketing campaign overlooked. No matter what you identify, you need to outline what you’ll try to remedy that gap. If time is of the essence, you might have to create a contingency plan.

For example, let’s assume you cannot install the necessary tracking. You may identify that you will need to correlate the data from other metrics, and the accuracy won’t be great but you know it’s good enough for this initial run. You’ll know that you need to socialize the deficiency in your analytics plan to key stakeholders so when you report on the findings there are not questions as to why certain data was omitted.

The Test & Learning plan is your way to cover all your bases. Use it as a tool to guide your decisions, but also guide the conversations you have with others on your team or within the company.
